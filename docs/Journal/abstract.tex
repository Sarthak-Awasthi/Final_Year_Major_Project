\begin{abstract}
This document is a running record of our MVP research project --- a single-player medieval village RPG that serves as a multi-agent RL research playground. The system combines: (1)~a hierarchical MDP for quest progression, (2)~tabular Q-learning agents with adaptive personalities and role-specific action masking that give NPCs autonomous behavior, (3)~a decomposed reward model (penalty $+$ individual $+ \lambda \cdot$ community) with a dynamic cooperation feedback loop, (4)~a system shock engine that creates non-stationary environments for studying agent adaptation, and (5)~an optional provider-backed LLM (Ollama/OpenAI-compatible) that enriches narration and dialogue. The game is fully playable as a web app (Python/FastAPI backend, vanilla JS frontend with Chart.js analytics dashboard). The research hypothesis is that NPC policies should shift from individual gains toward cooperative behavior over time, especially under system shocks. This document covers the math behind the systems, what we've implemented, key design decisions, and what we're planning next. It's meant as a reference for our weekly meetings --- not a polished paper.
\end{abstract}
